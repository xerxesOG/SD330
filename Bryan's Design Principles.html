Bryan's Design Principles

Key Principles

  • Visibility
  • Consistency
  • Feedback
  • Constraints
  • Affordance

Complexity Considerations

  • The law of Conservation of Complexity: For any system, there is a certain amount of complexity which cannot be reduced
  • Use icons, but ensure their meaning is obvious
  • Provide only essential choices to prevent users from feeling overwhelmed, thereby improving the decision-making process.
  • Creating interfaces that users can interact with immediately, learning as they go rather than requiring up-front instruction.
  • Break down complex tasks into simpler, manageable modules.
  • Be as easy as email.
  • Necessity of complexity: Complexity is necessary when an app has a lot of functionality and when the designer want to simplify user's interaction

Cultural Considerations

  • Universalims Vs Paricularism: Do the users place value on rules, laws, and dogma or they believe the world to be circumstantial?
  • Individualism Vs Communitarianism: Do the users believe in in personal freedom and achievement or the group is greater than the individual?
  • Specific Vs Diffuse: Are work and personal lives kepts separate or they overlap?
  • Neutral Vs Emotional: Do the users make great efforts to express their emotions or to keep them in control?
  • Achievement Vs Ascription: Are the usersr valued for what they do or who they are?
  • Sequential time Vs Synchronous time: Do the users believe that events happen in a striated sequence or the past, present, and future are an interwoven continuum?
  • Internal direction Vs Outer direction: Do the users profess to control nature and environment or the opposite?
  • Ensure that interfaces supports multiple languages and is culturally appropriate for different regions.
  • Be aware that certain symbols might have different meanings across cultures. Always test icons and symbols with the target audience to ensure they're perceived as intended.

Conceptional Considerations

  • Design the conceptional model purposely, do not let it arise from the technology
  • Make sure the mental model and the conceptional model match
  • Build user stories or scenarios to represent and understand your users' needs, actions, and the experiences you want to provide.
  • Rely on familiar interface patterns to provide a sense of familiarity to users.
  • Jakob's law: users spend most of their time on other sites. This means that usrs prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they already know
  • When making changes, minimize discord by empowering users to continue using a familiar version for a limited time.

Cognitive Design Principles

  • Attention
    • People may not see what you think your desing is showing them
    • How you organize your user interface is important
    • People pay attention only to salient clues
  • Perception
    • People scan screens based on past experience and expectations
    • It is easier to recognize information than to recall it
  • Memory
    • The more attention tha is paid to somethind, the more likely it is to be remembered
    • Good names that help the user remember information
    • Search and find tools to help the user with searching.

Human Centered Design Principles

  • Provide discoverability: can a user determine what actions are possible?
  • Regularly test your design with real users to gather feedback and insights.
  • Utilize a conceptional model
  • Adopt an iterative design approach, making improvements based on user feedback and retesting
  • Include affordance, signifiers, constraints
  • Ensure you have good mappings- the relationship between controls and their actions

Emotional/ Accessibility/ Errors Principles

  • Have empathy for our users
  • Make feedback immediate
  • Ensure that color choices are accessible to those with color vision deficiencies.
  • Provide clear error messages that guide users on how to resolve the issue, rather than just indicating that an error occurred.

DevOps Principles

  • Continous Integration: Every day developers are committing codes in a shared repository which is a good development practice.
  • Continous Delivery: Any software should be in a releasable state throughout its cycle
  • Implement automated testing to ensure that changes to the code don't introduce new errors.
  • Manage infrastructure using code and automation, which allows consistent and reliable provisioning of resources.
  • Establish mechanisms for fast feedback on the development process, enabling quicker response times to issues.
  • Continous Deployment: Every change in each of its developmentphase should pass all automated tests at the time of production.
  • Value Stream Mapping: A lean tool that helps in depicting the entire flow of information, material, and works across functional silos including quality and time
  • Theory of Constraints: A methodology for identifying the most limiting factor to achieve a milestone and then systematically improving the constraint until it is no longer the limiting factor
